Get your documents ready

You may need information from some or all of these documents when you create a profile:

  • a passport or travel document
    • If your passport expires within 6 months from the day you apply, you should renew it. When you get your new passport, update your passport information in your application.
    • Venezuelan passport holders: You may need to complete extra steps when you create a profile or fill out your application.
  • language test results
  • proof of Canadian education or an educational credential assessment report for immigration purposes if:
    • you’re applying through the Federal Skilled Workers Program, or
    • you want to get points for the education you got outside Canada
  • written job offer from an employer in Canada (if you have one)
  • proof of work experience (such as a reference or experience letter from an employer)
  • certificate of qualification in a trade occupation issued by a Canadian province or territory (if you have one)
  • proof of funds
  • police certificates
  • provincial nomination (if you applied for the Provincial Nominee Program)

If the printed expiry date on your Venezuelan passport has passed

Follow these steps, if the printed expiry date has passed, and you’re creating a profile or submitting an application under Express Entry:

  1. Add 5 years to the printed expiry date shown on your passport and enter it in the expiry date field when creating your profile or submitting your online application.
  2. Include a letter of explanation with your application stating: “I am a Venezuelan national with a Venezuelan passport, which has been extended for five years.”

Warning: If your passport is still expired after adding 5 years to the printed expiry date, you cannot use it to create a profile or submit an application through Express Entry.
